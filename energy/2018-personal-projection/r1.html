What is a personal projection?

We take how much you are paying now and project this figure twelve months into the future. This way you’ll be able to find a tariff that is cheaper than your current one.

Note: Because of the general state of the energy market you may not find a tariff cheapaer than your current one. However we can help you find the cheapest tariff on the market currently.

How do other price comparison websites calculate personal projection?

If your current tariff ends in less than 12 months Ofgem requires every comparison website to assume that you do nothing and are automatically moved to your supplier’s Standard Variable Tariff, which is always much more expensive. Comparison sites are then required to calculate your personal projection for the next 12 months using a mix of your current tariff and the Standard Variable Tariff.

Why is this wrong?

We disagree with this guidance, and here’s why: if you switch from a tariff that cost you £1000 per year to a tariff that will cost you £800 per year you save exactly £200.

However, if a comparison website inflates your 12 months cost from £1000 to £1200 by assuming you’ll move to a Standard Variable Tariff and then uses this inflated personal projection to show you savings it may seem like you are saving £400.

That is why we choose to be honest with our customers and calculate all your future costs using your current tariff only. This should give you a realistic picture of what you are paying now, and how much you’re likely to pay in the future.
