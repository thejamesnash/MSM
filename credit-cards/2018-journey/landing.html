Balance transfers Thanks for that – we think a 0% balance transfer card can help you. This is where you get a new card, then ‘do a balance transfer’ from it to your old card (usually for a small one-off fee), meaning the balance on the old card is cleared, and you owe the new card, but at 0%. This means more of your repayments clear the debt, rather than just paying off the interest, so you can be debt free quicker, and often save £100s or even £1,000s in interest. Money transfers Sounds odd, but some cards do let you do a money transfer – where you transfer cash off the card into your bank account (for a small one-off fee), meaning you owe the card provider. Once you have the money in your bank account, you can use it as if it was a loan – maybe to pay off an overdraft or payday loan with higher interest, or maybe to buy something where the vendor can’t (or won’t) take payment by credit card. Usually these cards give you a 0% period, which it’s best to use to budget to pay the card off. Plus, you’ll still need to make at least the minimum payment each month to keep the 0% deal.
